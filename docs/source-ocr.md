# Source OCR — Patient Animal Resource binder

Transcribed from 10 photos (IMG_9691–IMG_9700.DNG) of a physical 3-ring binder.
Originals + JPEG conversions kept under `_source/` (git-ignored, large raw files).

The binder is a reference tool for a hospital ("MCH") program that helps **hospitalized
patients arrange care for the animals they leave at home**. It pairs a two-page workflow
("the two-pager") with tabbed reference sections (Animal Control Officers, Pet Food
Pantries, Kennels) and a full copy of the relevant Maine statute (the "legal section").

---

## 1. The two-pager — Patient Animal Resource Information (IMG_9691, IMG_9692)

**Mission:** to provide care resources and information about animals when the patient is in
the hospital. In the process, this can help alleviate stress on patients and provide
resources to ensure everyone involved is safe. This includes working with local resources
and community.

→ MCH provides care to patients from different towns and counties, and this app will provide
a listing of agencies that can help, making this an easier transition for everyone.

### Step 1: Intake
a. Upon intake, having a conversation with patients regarding animals at home. This includes
   questions such as:
   - Do you have an animal at home?
   - Do you have a family taking care of it, or a caretaker?
   - Do you have a POA? (in case patient is unable to make a decision)
b. After conversation, obtain consent for phone numbers (consent form to allow us to
   contact). If they do not have any help and they have animals at home, would they be
   willing to sign a consent for us to find help for them, such as contacting animal control
   officers for transporting animals to kennel, or whoever patient consents to have animal
   care.
c. If resources are needed, a list of kennels and shelters are listed within the app that
   could aid.
   1. One goal would be to establish a fund for people who meet the criteria to be able to
      access monies for help.
   2. Establish a volunteer network to help with fundraising, transport, and food and in this
      [section].

### Step 2: If they want to relinquish care
a. A consent form will be provided for signing the rights of the animal over and creates less
   barriers for animal control and police to properly obtain possession of the animal and
   make sure it is humanely transported to humane society (or veterinarian if needed).
a. (cont. on IMG_9692) If a caretaker or person needs help with resources for feeding the
   animal, there is a list of food pantries and shelters that always have extra supply for
   people in need.

### Step 3: Food Resources
- List of food pantries / shelters with extra supply.

**Reference Note:** Large animals, such as livestock → There is Maine Society of Protection
and Abuse in Windham [Animal Refuge League of Greater Portland / "Maine Society for the
Protection of Animals," Windham] that helps people at all levels.

Consent forms will be provided in the app.
